Optimal Portfolio Allocations

Minimum Variance Portfolio

(Lowest Risk)

This portfolio is designed for maximum safety by minimising volatility. It's a good fit for a conservative investor whose primary goal is capital preservation over high growth.

Maximum Sharpe Ratio Portfolio

(Best Risk-Adjusted Return)

This is the tangency portfolio - it offers the best possible return for the amount of risk taken and forms the basis of the Capital Market Line. It's considered the most efficient portfolio.

Maximum Sortino Ratio Portfolio

(Best Downside-Risk Return)

This portfolio maximizes return against "bad" volatility (downside risk). It's useful for investors who care more about losses than overall volatility.

Minimum CVaR Portfolio

(Lowest Tail Risk)

This portfolio minimises Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR), aiming to reduce the average loss in the worst-case scenarios (e.g., the worst 5% of outcomes). Ideal for highly risk-averse investors.
